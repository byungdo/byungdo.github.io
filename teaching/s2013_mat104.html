Midterm-I: February 26th(Tuesday), In-class.
Since there is no allocated lesson in the course calendar, We will not have a review session before the first midterm exam. However, the following information will be sufficient for preparation.
- All problems will be chosen identically from assigned homework.

- 15 questions will be asked, and there will be no partial credits.
 - Based on the course calendar, from Lesson 1 to Lesson 6 will be covered.
- Related sample final exam problems are: #1[2.7], #2[3.6], #12[3.2]

Midterm-III: April 23th(Tuesday), In-class.
To assist you to be prepared for the departmental final exam, I will make this exam as the following:
- It will be a cumulative exam. i.e. the exam will cover from Lesson 1 to Lesson 20 based on the course calendar.
- All problems will be of one of the type in the sample final exam.
- Problems will not be identical from homework, since it is obvious that the uniform final exam problems will not be identical from the homework I assigned.
- 15 questions will be asked, and there will be no partial credits.
Be aware that if you cannot receive a score greater than or equal to 9/15, you are very likely to fail in the departmental final exam unless you do not put a special attention in studying this subject.
Here are Problems and Solutions. The average of enrolled students was 3.5/15(23.1%), the average of examinees was 5.2/15(34.7%), and the maximum score achieved by a student was 9/15(60.0%).
